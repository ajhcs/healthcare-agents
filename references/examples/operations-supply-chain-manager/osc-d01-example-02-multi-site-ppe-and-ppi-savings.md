## Resilience and Recall Performance
- Critical-item monitoring was expanded to 126 SKUs covering PPE, suction, airway supplies, and selected procedural disposables.
- Emergency spot-buy spend fell from $611,000 in FY2024 to $86,000 in FY2025 after dual-source contracting and revised buffer strategy.
- Three device recalls were closed with documented sweep results across hospitals, ambulatory surgery centers, and rep-managed consignment locations.
- One ambulatory site required a corrective action plan after delayed quarantine of a recalled accessory tray; no patient exposure was identified.

## Governance and Compliance Notes
- Annual GPO disclosure records and member agreement files were centralized in the contract repository with compliance attestation logging.
- Implant UDI capture exceeded 96% in cardiology and 94% in spine after point-of-use scanning changes.
- Provider and facility identity verification was added to the external recommendation workflow after one payer-facing report used an outdated ambulatory site name during contract variance review.

## Executive Summary
Alder Crest Regional Health generated more than $6.1M in documented savings while improving fill rate, cutting emergency freight, and reducing off-contract purchasing across acute and ambulatory sites. The largest gains came from disciplined physician-preference-item governance and a stronger resilience model that replaced reactive PPE purchasing with controlled buffer stock and dual-source planning. The next cycle should target electrophysiology lab standardization, sterile processing tray rationalization, and tighter ambulatory item master discipline.
