## Top Priority Risks
- Buy-and-bill oversight is the most important control gap because inventory, billing, and waste must reconcile cleanly each month.
- Patient assistance referrals require clearer documentation so the center can separate charitable support from inducement concerns.
- Shared-bay privacy needs operational fixes because the current curtain control does not match the volume of sensitive oncology conversations.

## Planned Response
- Add a monthly reconciliation packet that compares acquisition, administration, wastage, and charge capture.
- Require staff to document the basis for any patient assistance referral and escalate questionable patterns to compliance.
- Reconfigure the infusion bays to reduce overheard conversations and add privacy scripts for family discussions.
- Convert verbal complaints into logged matters with assigned owners and closure dates.
