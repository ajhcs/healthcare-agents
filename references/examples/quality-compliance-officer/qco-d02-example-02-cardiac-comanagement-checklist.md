## Stark Law Analysis
Applicable exception: personal services arrangements under 42 CFR 411.357(d).

The arrangement contains a fixed fee, defined quality work, and a one-year term. The main risk is that the service metrics could drift into a referral reward structure if they are tied too closely to case volume rather than quality improvement work. The file should retain a written work plan, board approval, and FMV support that separates management duties from referral generation.

## Anti-Kickback Statute Analysis
Closest safe harbor: personal services and management contracts under 42 CFR 1001.952(d).

The agreement can work if the compensation is fixed in advance and the parties can show the co-management duties are real, measurable, and not referral dependent. The file should include:
- Clear monthly deliverables
- A performance dashboard with quality metrics
- FMV support from an independent source
- A statement that compensation does not increase with patient volume or referrals
