# Annual Compliance Risk Assessment

**Organization**: Pine Ridge Critical Access Hospital

## Methodology
This assessment reviewed transfer logs, ED triage times, telehealth incident notes, workstation access reports, and exclusion-screening results. The scoring framework used likelihood, impact, and control maturity. The regulatory baseline included EMTALA at 42 CFR Part 489.24, HIPAA privacy and security at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164, and the OIG General Compliance Program Guidance issued in November 2023.

## Top Priority Risks
- Transfer documentation is the most urgent risk because delayed transfers can intersect with EMTALA obligations.
- Access control requires process redesign because the issue occurs at predictable handoff times.
- Screening coverage must be expanded to temporary staff because the current roster is too narrow.

## Planned Response
- Require transfer packets to be complete before transport and track defects weekly.
- Add a mandatory login timeout and end-of-shift workstation sweep.
- Expand screening to cover all temporary and contracted staff before first shift.
- Review telehealth consent language for consistency and re-train front-desk staff on identity verification.
