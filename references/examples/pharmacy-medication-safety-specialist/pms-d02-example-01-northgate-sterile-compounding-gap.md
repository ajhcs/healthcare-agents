# USP 797/800 Compliance Gap Analysis

**Facility**: Northgate Outpatient Oncology Pharmacy  
**Assessment Date**: March 6, 2026  
**Assessor**: Lina Patel, PharmD, BCPS  
**Compounding Category**: Both

## USP 797 Assessment
### Facility & Engineering Controls
- ISO Class 5 PEC was certified on time, but the ante-area pressure log showed three missed weekend entries.
- ISO Class 7 buffer area certification was current, but the temperature excursion log lacked follow-up signatures.
- Cleanroom access was restricted, but gowning supplies were stored inside the ante-area and contaminated the workflow.

### Personnel
- Initial competency was complete for pharmacists, but two technicians had expired media fill documentation.
- Garbing and gloving observation was documented for the lead technician only.
- Written knowledge checks were current, but the training file did not show annual reassessment for temporary staff.

### Environmental Monitoring
- Viable air sampling was overdue by 17 days.
- Surface sampling results were available, but action-level excursions were not tied to CAPA records.
- Gloved fingertip sampling was missing for one new pharmacist.

### Documentation
- Master formulation records existed for all routine CSPs.
- Compounding records were complete for 22 of 25 recent batches.
- Beyond-use dating assignments were inconsistent for refrigerated batches prepared late in the day.

## USP 800 Assessment
### Hazardous Drug List
- The facility HD list existed, but it had not been reconciled against the current NIOSH list.
- Several oral antineoplastic agents were omitted from the handling list.

### Engineering Controls
- A C-PEC was present, but the negative-pressure C-SEC certificate had expired 5 months earlier.
- Hazardous drug receiving occurred in a general receiving bay instead of the designated HD unpacking area.

### PPE & Safety
- Chemotherapy-tested gloves were stocked, but fit-testing records were incomplete.
- Spill kits existed, but staff could not identify the location of the HD spill kit during the walk-through.
