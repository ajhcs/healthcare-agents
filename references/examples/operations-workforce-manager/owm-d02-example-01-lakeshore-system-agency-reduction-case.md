Lakeshore Valley’s contract labor burden is concentrated in ICU, med-surg, and ED. Current annual agency spend of $18.6M equals 7.8% of total nursing labor cost and is driven more by vacancy duration and night-shift attrition than by pure volume growth. Internal fully loaded RN cost remains materially below traveler bill rates even after float differential, recruitment cost, and benefit load are included.

The highest-yield action is float pool expansion. A 34 FTE pool sized across critical care, med-surg/telemetry, and ED clusters absorbs daily census variation, LOA coverage, and open-shift instability that currently pushes units to agency requests. The proposed pool is modeled to displace 31.5 agency FTEs with positive first-year savings after training and differential expense.

Retention investments are necessary to keep agency reduction durable. Night-shift turnover is 1.8 times day-shift turnover in the adult acute-care service lines. Career ladder progression, manager span-of-control relief, and protected preceptor pay are expected to reduce voluntary RN turnover by 2.6 percentage points over twelve months, which lowers both replacement cost and premium backfill.

Financially, the plan generates $6.22M in annualized net savings at steady state and lowers premium pay ratio from 17.1% to 12.9%. Operationally, it improves continuity, reduces orientation churn on high-acuity units, and supports safer staffing coverage.
