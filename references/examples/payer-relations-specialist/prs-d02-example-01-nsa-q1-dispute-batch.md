## Operational Findings
- Emergency claims were paid at initial levels that tracked well below the provider's selected offers in IDR.
- SummitGate resolved most cases in open negotiation, but its settlement values stayed compressed near the QPA floor.
- Northline Select showed the highest number of ineligible batches because related-service grouping was inconsistent across facilities.
- Two facility-based cases were flagged because consent packets were signed after service, which weakened the balance-billing position.
- The dispute queue moved fastest when claim-level coding, remittance dates, and notice timestamps were normalized before portal submission.

## Recommendations
- Tighten batching rules and require same-payer, same-service, same-location validation before filing.
- Prioritize IDR on emergency anesthesia and radiology claims where the spread between payment and target offer is widest.
- Rework the facility intake workflow so notice-and-consent review happens before scheduling for any elective OON case.
- Track payer-specific negotiation outcomes monthly and escalate the lowest-paying payers into contract strategy review.
