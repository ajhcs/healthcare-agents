# Throughput Improvement Business Case

**Initiative**: Discharge Lounge and Hospitality Team Launch
**Sponsor**: Celia North, Chief Operating Officer
**Date**: 2026-02-18
**Projected Go-Live**: 2026-06-01

## Current State
- Current occupancy rate: 88.4%
- Current ALOS: 4.9 days (vs. benchmark: 4.4 days)
- Current DBN rate: 31%
- Annual ED boarding hours: 18,240
- Current bed turnaround time: 74 minutes
- Estimated annual revenue lost to capacity constraints: $3,480,000

## Proposed Intervention
Launch a 10-chair discharge lounge adjacent to the main lobby with dedicated staffing from 09:00 to 21:00, standardized discharge criteria, pharmacy-to-chair med delivery, and transport coordination. The lounge will accept medically cleared adult patients awaiting prescriptions, family pickup, ride-share arrival, or post-discharge paperwork. Bed release will occur at patient transfer to the lounge rather than curb departure.

- Total annual cost: $650,000 recurring operating cost plus $450,000 one-time implementation cost
- Net annual benefit: $1,510,000 after the first year operating run rate
- Payback period: 8.7 months
- 3-year ROI: 223%

## Operating Assumptions
- 14 discharge-ready patients per day meet lounge criteria, with 8 daily transfers in the first 90 days and 10 daily transfers by month 6.
- Each timely lounge transfer releases a staffed bed 70 minutes sooner than current curb-to-bed-release practice.
- 1 released bed equivalent supports 360 incremental medical/surgical admissions per year at existing case mix.
- Pharmacy bedside delivery will cover 65% of lounge discharges, reducing late departure related to prescription pickup.

## Risk Assessment
| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Clinical teams send patients before discharge readiness is fully complete | M | H | Hard eligibility criteria, charge nurse sign-off, exclusion list for oxygen titration, unstable vitals, and unresolved home service needs |
| Lounge becomes a holding area with no transport resolution | M | M | Dedicated transport coordinator, ride-estimated-time tracking, escalation at 60 minutes |
| Family dissatisfaction with relocation after discharge order | L | M | Standard scripts, concierge support, visible comfort amenities, and physician reinforcement during rounding |
| Pharmacy turnaround remains slow | M | M | Meds-to-beds priority queue and noon cutover metric review with pharmacy director |

## Implementation Milestones
- March 2026: Final design approval, space release, ADT build charter.
- April 2026: Construction start, policy approval, staffing requisitions posted.
- May 2026: Staff hired, workflow simulation completed, pilot on two med/surg units.
- June 2026: Full go-live Monday through Sunday, 09:00 to 21:00.

## Governance and Compliance
- Discharge readiness and transition planning remain under the medical team and case management; the lounge accelerates bed release only after discharge requirements are complete.
- Discharge planning workflows remain aligned with 42 CFR 482.43 and existing patient-rights escalation processes.
- Monthly review will include readmissions within 7 days, patient complaints tied to discharge experience, and any missed handoff defects.

## Recommendation
Approve capital and staffing for a June 2026 launch. The initiative is low-complexity operationally, uses existing patient flow capability, and creates six effective staffed-bed equivalents without opening a new unit. If results miss target by more than 15% after 90 days, reduce hours to weekday peak periods and redeploy one technician FTE.
