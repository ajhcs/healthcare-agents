## Deficiency Response
### F689 — Free of Accident Hazards/Supervision/Devices
**Scope/Severity**: G
**Regulatory Cite**: 42 CFR § 483.25(d)

**Corrective Action**:
1. How the facility will correct the specific deficiency cited:
   Resident R17 sustained two unwitnessed falls on 2026-02-28 and 2026-03-06, with the second event resulting in a left orbital bruise and transfer to the emergency department. On 2026-03-17 the interdisciplinary team completed an immediate reassessment of fall risk, revised the care plan, initiated low bed and floor mats, added supervised evening toileting every two hours, changed transfer status to one-person assist with gait belt, and re-educated direct-care staff assigned to the resident. The attending physician and resident representative were notified on 2026-03-17. The revised interventions were in place before close of business on 2026-03-17.

2. How the facility will identify other residents with potential to be affected:
   The DON, MDS Coordinator, and Rehab Director completed a house-wide audit of all current residents with one or more falls in the prior 30 days, all residents coded as requiring extensive or limited assist for transfers, all residents with cognitive impairment in Section C, and all residents with psychotropic use in Section N. The audit population totaled 46 residents and was completed on 2026-03-20. Each chart was reviewed for post-fall analysis, physician notification, care-plan revision, intervention implementation, and shift-to-shift communication. Residents with identified gaps received same-day corrective action.

3. What systemic changes the facility will make to prevent recurrence:
   The facility revised the post-fall protocol effective 2026-03-21 to require an interdisciplinary huddle within 12 hours of any fall, documentation of root-cause factors, and care-plan revision before the next scheduled shift change. The nurse manager now reviews all falls from the prior 24 hours at the morning clinical meeting. CNA assignment sheets were updated to display individualized fall interventions. Licensed nurses and CNAs on all shifts completed focused education on F689 requirements, resident-specific supervision, and documentation standards between 2026-03-18 and 2026-03-24. The EHR now includes a hard-stop requiring intervention review after a second fall within 14 days. Weekend management rounds were added to validate implementation of low beds, alarms, mats, and toileting plans.

4. How the facility will monitor the corrective action:
   The DON or designee will audit 5 residents per week with recent falls for 8 weeks, then 5 residents every other week for 8 additional weeks. Audit elements include timely post-fall huddle completion, physician and representative notification, care-plan revision, intervention implementation on the floor, and staff awareness of resident-specific precautions. Results will be reviewed at weekly QAPI for 2 months and monthly QAPI thereafter. Any audit below 95% compliance will trigger immediate staff re-education and repeat audit within 72 hours.
