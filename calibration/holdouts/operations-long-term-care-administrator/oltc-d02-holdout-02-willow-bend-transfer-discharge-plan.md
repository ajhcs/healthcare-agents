## Deficiency Response
### F623 — Notice Requirements Before Transfer/Discharge
**Scope/Severity**: D
**Regulatory Cite**: 42 CFR § 483.15(c)(5)-(6)

**Corrective Action**:
1. How the facility will correct the specific deficiency cited:
   Resident R09's transfer and discharge record was reviewed immediately by the Administrator, Social Services Director, and Admissions Coordinator. The responsible representative received a corrected notice packet with complete appeal rights information, ombudsman contact details, and documented counseling regarding hospital return rights. The resident record was updated to reflect the corrected communication and resident-rights review.

2. How the facility will identify other residents with potential to be affected:
   The facility reviewed all involuntary transfer and discharge notices issued during the prior 90 days, along with all current hospital-transfer records requiring bed-hold or return-right documentation. Each record was audited for notice content, timing, representative notification, ombudsman information, appeal language, and return-right documentation.

3. What systemic changes the facility will make to prevent recurrence:
   Effective 2026-01-12, all facility-initiated transfer and discharge actions require administrator review before notice issuance. A standardized checklist was added to the discharge packet to verify reason, timing, appeal protections, ombudsman details, physician documentation, and hospital return-right counseling. Social Services and Admissions staff completed focused education on F622, F623, and F626. The EHR discharge workflow now requires completion of the resident-rights checklist before final packet generation.

4. How the facility will monitor the corrective action:
   The Administrator or designee will audit every facility-initiated transfer or discharge notice for 60 days, then review monthly for 3 months. Audit elements include complete notice content, representative notification, ombudsman information, appeal rights, and hospital return-right documentation. Findings will be trended at QAPI, and any deficient packet will be corrected immediately with retraining of involved staff.
