## Deficiency Response
### F758 — Free From Unnecessary Psychotropic Medications/PRN Use
**Scope/Severity**: E
**Regulatory Cite**: 42 CFR § 483.45(e)

**Corrective Action**:
1. How the facility will correct the specific deficiency cited:
   Resident R22's PRN quetiapine order and supporting documentation were reviewed immediately with the attending practitioner, consultant pharmacist, Director of Nursing, and Social Services. The order status, physician rationale, target symptoms, nonpharmacologic interventions, and monitoring record were brought into compliance, and the care plan was revised to reflect current behavioral supports and medication oversight.

2. How the facility will identify other residents with potential to be affected:
   The facility completed a psychotropic medication review of all current residents receiving antipsychotics, anxiolytics, hypnotics, antidepressants used for behavioral symptom management, and PRN psychotropic orders. The review focused on documented indication, target symptoms, monitoring, gradual dose reduction review when applicable, PRN duration controls, pharmacist irregularity reporting, and physician response.

3. What systemic changes the facility will make to prevent recurrence:
   A psychotropic oversight tracker is now maintained jointly by the DON, consultant pharmacist, and Medical Director. Every psychotropic order requires a documented indication, target symptoms, behavior-monitoring pathway when applicable, review of nonpharmacologic interventions, and follow-up timing. PRN psychotropic orders are flagged for stop-date review before they exceed allowed short-duration parameters. Monthly pharmacist findings must show practitioner response and facility follow-through. Licensed nurses, unit managers, and social services staff completed focused education on F757/F758 requirements, documentation of behavioral interventions, and medication review escalation.

4. How the facility will monitor the corrective action:
   The DON or designee will audit all new and active psychotropic orders weekly for 6 weeks, then monthly for 3 months. Audit elements include indication, monitoring, care-plan alignment, pharmacist recommendation follow-up, and prescriber review. Findings will be reported to QAPI and the Medical Director. Any missed element will require immediate correction and re-audit within 5 business days.

**Completion Date**: 2026-02-19
