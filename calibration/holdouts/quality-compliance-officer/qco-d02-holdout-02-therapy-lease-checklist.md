# Financial Arrangement Compliance Review

**Arrangement Type**: Combined space and equipment lease  
**Parties**: Cedar Grove Therapy Clinic and Summit Lease Holdings  
**Referral Relationship**: Yes. The clinic treats Medicare beneficiaries and sends some patients to physician-owned referral sources.  
**Date Reviewed**: 2026-04-09  
**Reviewed By**: Maya Chen, CHC

## Stark Law Analysis
Applicable exception: space rental at 42 CFR 411.357(a) and equipment rental at 42 CFR 411.357(b).

The written term is one year and the rent is fixed monthly, which is a good start. The risk is that the leased suite is larger than reasonably necessary for the therapy program and the equipment list includes items that are not clearly used by the clinic. The file should document square footage, equipment inventory, FMV support, and why the space is commercially reasonable even if no referral volume exists.

## Anti-Kickback Statute Analysis
Closest safe harbor: space rental and equipment rental under 42 CFR 1001.952(b).

The agreement requires more than a signature page to be safe. The clinic should confirm:
- The space is physically specified and exclusively controlled during the lease term
- The rental charge is set in advance and tied to FMV
- The equipment is limited to items the clinic actually uses
- No side payments, free storage, or hidden service credits are bundled into the deal

Without those controls, the arrangement is moderately risky and should not be approved until the file is cleaned up.
