# Annual Compliance Risk Assessment

**Organization**: Northgate Behavioral Health Hospital  
**Assessment Period**: Fiscal Year 2026  
**Compliance Officer**: Maya Chen, CHC  
**Date Completed**: 2026-04-09  
**Approved By**: Compliance Committee on 2026-04-09

## Methodology
This assessment reviewed seclusion logs, transfer delay reports, hotline summaries, privacy incident reports, and the monthly exclusion-screening log. Risk scoring used a one-to-five scale for likelihood and impact, then adjusted for existing control maturity. The analysis was grounded in EMTALA requirements at 42 CFR Part 489.24, HIPAA privacy and security requirements at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164, and the OIG General Compliance Program Guidance issued in November 2023.

## Top Priority Risks
- EMTALA transfer handling requires immediate remediation because delay patterns involve emergency department patients who may still have an emergency medical condition under 42 CFR Part 489.24.
- HIPAA workstation access requires physical workflow changes, not just retraining, because the current control fails during handoffs and shift changes.
- Retaliation risk must be addressed through manager coaching and board visibility because hotline silence will hide the next material issue.

## Planned Response
- Standardize transfer documentation to require receiving-facility acceptance, physician certification, and timestamped handoff notes before transport.
- Replace shared workstation practices with automatic screen-lock timeout, end-of-shift closeout checks, and spot audits during the evening shift.
- Add contractor and agency staff to the screening roster and tie monthly attestation to scheduling approval.
- Report quarterly to the board with trend analysis, corrective actions, and open items that remain above medium residual risk.
