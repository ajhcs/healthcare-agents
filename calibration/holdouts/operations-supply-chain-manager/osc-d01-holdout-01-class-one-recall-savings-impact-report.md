## Recall and Substitution Impact
- A Class I recall involving a vascular closure device triggered immediate quarantine across two cath labs, one hybrid OR, and central supply.
- Recall-response freight and temporary substitute price premium were tracked as operational mitigation and excluded from realized savings.
- The alternate contracted product stabilized case flow within 48 hours and prevented prolonged off-contract leakage.
- Quarantine, return coordination, and substitute-item setup were completed through the recall command workflow with documented closeout.

## Finance and Compliance Notes
- Savings calculations used verified price reductions multiplied by purchased volume after go-live dates.
- Avoided case cancellations, emergency preparedness buffer preservation, and recall-response mitigation were tracked separately from realized savings.
- Recall documentation included notice intake, location sweeps, quarantine counts, return status, and substitute-product activation.
- Annual GPO disclosure and contract-governance files were logged with compliance review.

## Executive Summary
Silver Birch Care Network delivered $2.95M in documented savings while managing a high-risk recall event without overstating operational mitigation as savings. The year showed improved contract compliance, stronger inventory control, and better discipline in category standardization. The next cycle should focus on cath lab physician-preference items, lower-cost substitute readiness planning, and tighter freight governance for urgent procedural products.
