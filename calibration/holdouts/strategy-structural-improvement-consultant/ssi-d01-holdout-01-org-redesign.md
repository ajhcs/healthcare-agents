# Organizational Redesign Proposal

The organization needs a cleaner structure, not just a new chart. The proposal should show which layers disappear, which roles change, and how governance stays intact while accountability gets clearer.

## Strategic Context
Harbor Crest Regional Health System has grown through acquisition and now needs a structure that can manage five hospitals, two ambulatory divisions, and a centralized shared-services platform without adding another layer of confusion.

## Proposed Organizational Structure
| Layer | Current State | Proposed State |
|---|---|---|
| Executive layer | 7 direct reports | 5 direct reports with clearer portfolios |
| Hospital leadership | Site-specific silos | Site leader plus service-line dyad |
| Shared services | Fragmented across campuses | Centralized with local service agreements |

## Key Design Decisions
- Reduce the span of control at the top by collapsing duplicate vice president roles.
- Adopt a dyad structure for service lines that need clinical and operational alignment.
- Pull finance, HR, and supply chain into a common shared-services tower with local service expectations.

## Role Changes
| Role | Current / New | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Hospital COO | Recast as site integration leader | Focus the role on local execution rather than duplicate strategy work |
| Service line director | New or expanded | Own cross-site performance and physician alignment |
| Shared services leader | Expanded | Standardize support functions across campuses |

## Governance Implications
- The board should retain reserve powers on capital, mergers, and major service changes.
- Management should receive explicit authority for staffing, daily operations, and vendor execution within approved limits.
- The redesign must preserve a clean escalation path for compliance and patient safety issues.

## Change Management Plan
- Publish the new org chart and decision-rights summary together so the structure is not interpreted as cosmetic.
- Sequence leadership announcements before role changes so reporting confusion does not spread into operations.
- Keep the first ninety days focused on role clarity, not on forcing a full cultural reset.

## Risk Assessment
| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Middle-manager resistance | Medium | Use transition coaching and explicit role maps |
| Decision drift | Medium | Document escalation thresholds and reserve powers |
| Cultural fatigue | High | Sequence changes and limit simultaneous reorganizations |

## Financial Impact
| Item | Annual Impact | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Duplicate leadership positions | $860,000 annual savings | Capture only after role changes are complete |
| Transition support and consulting | $320,000 one-time cost | Budget for design and rollout |
| Reduced management duplication | $540,000 annual run-rate | Cascades into shared services |

The redesign follows 42 CFR 482.12 governance expectations, IRS 501(r) discipline where community benefit sits in the background, and the practical reality that structure only matters if decision rights and escalation paths are explicit. The recommended shape reduces confusion at the point of work while preserving a clear line of accountability to the board and the executive team.
