# Agency Staff Reduction Business Case

**Organization**: Redwood Coast Medical Center
**Date**: 2026-04-09
**Current Annual Agency Spend**: $14.2M

## Implementation Timeline
| Phase | Months | Milestones | Expected Agency Reduction |
|-------|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| 1 — Quick wins | 1-3 | Freeze off-contract usage, offer conversion to current travelers, launch telemetry night premium | -12% |
| 2 — Float pool | 3-6 | Hire and orient centralized float team for med-surg, telemetry, ED cluster | -19% |
| 3 — Retention | 6-12 | Manager scorecards, career ladder rollout, residency preceptor support | -15% |
| 4 — Sustained | 12-18 | Float pool maturity and specialty cross-training | Target: 4.5% agency |

Redwood Coast’s traveler burden is no longer a temporary spike. It is a structural response to telemetry growth, uneven night-shift retention, and insufficient internal flex capacity. The strongest business case is a float pool sized to daily variability rather than a repeated sequence of emergency agency requests.

The case should present first-year positive economics while preserving shift-level acuity coverage and competency discipline. Quality oversight should follow falls, rapid response volume, medication-event patterns, and missed-break rates as agency dependence declines.
